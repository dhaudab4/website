As it is, travelling to a foreign contry is an adventures, however things are not the same as your home country interms of laws, culture, politics, currency and more therefore we are providing you some information that you need to know before coming to Ghana for the any visit. you need the following first before coming here

    Passport

    To be legally allowed to travel, whether Land (Bus) or Air (flight), all foreign nationals who wished to vists Ghana must have passports from their governments before they can enter the country.

    Visas

    Foreign nationals arriving in Ghana from ECOWAS Countries are not required to obtain visas since Ghana is a member of the ECOWAS and subscribes to the protocol on free movement. Non-ECOWAS nationals, however, require a visa.

    Resident Permit

    Residential permits (renewable annually) are processed by the Ghana Immigration Office when they have fully settled. The process to attain this is as follows:
  • Passport
  • Visa
  • Resident Permit

Residential permits (renewable annually) are processed by the Ghana Immigration Office when they have fully settled. The process to attain this is as follows:

Step 1: Procure your Non-Citizen Ghana Card (NCGC)

The application fee is $ 120
Annual renewal of NCGC is $ 60
NB: Applicant must be present at the office of the National Identification Authority with his/her passport.
Step 2: Requirements for Resident Permit (RRP)
A copy of student admission letter
A copy of biodata page of passport
A copy of the recent receipt of fees paid
A copy of NCGC
1 passport size picture
Applicants passport
Step 3: Cost of application for RRP
Gh¢ 150 for Ecowas Countries
Gh¢ 200 for Non-Ecowas Countries
NB: Failure to renew permits on said date will attract a penalty of Gh¢ 60 for Ecowas Countries and Gh¢ 80 for Non-Ecowas Countries.
